Love Letter

#sentiments 
January 2, 2018

Dear stranger, I know we’ve just met but, I love you.

I love you more than you could know. I love you unconditionally. I love you.

I cannot know your pain, your experience, Your suffering. That is all yours. But you do not have to bear it alone.

Dance with melancholy And sing out your blues but please Don’t stop.

Talk to me. Tell me about it. Whatever you feel, feel it. Then do one thing for yourself–– One act of self-love.

It won’t always feel good Like going to the gym Or eating your vegetables Or calling your mother Or crying–– Crying helps.

But know this: Admitting weakness is not weak. It is simply the beginning of something new.

So tear up and throw out Those old pictures of your lover Into your favorite trash bin.

Forgive them and forgive yourself. And when you’re done you can begin Loving again. Loving yourself. Loving life.

We may feel just an ounce more ready to engage in a world filled with facts and problems, awe and inspiration, rosebuds and thorns, heartbreak and love.

We may feel just an ounce more ready to let ourselves be hurt again.
